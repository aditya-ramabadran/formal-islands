First Dirichlet eigenfunction via the Rayleigh quotient

First Dirichlet eigenfunction via the Rayleigh quotient. Let \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a bounded domain with sufficiently regular boundary. Define \[ \lambda_1 := \inf\left\{ \int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx :u\in H_0^1(\Omega),\ \int_\Omega u^2\,dx = 1 \right\}. \] Then the infimum is attained: there exists \(u_1\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) with \[ \int_\Omega u_1^2\,dx=1 \] such that \[ \lambda_1=\int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx. \] Moreover, \(u_1\) satisfies the weak eigenvalue equation \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u_1\cdot\nabla\varphi\,dx = \lambda_1 \int_\Omega u_1\varphi\,dx \qquad \forall \varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega). \]

Informal statement:

There exists \(u_1\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) such that \(\int_\Omega u_1^2\,dx=1\), \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx=\lambda_1\), and \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u_1\cdot\nabla\varphi\,dx = \lambda_1\int_\Omega u_1\varphi\,dx \qquad \forall \varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega). \]

Informal proof:

Choose a minimizing sequence \((u_n)\subset H_0^1(\Omega)\) with \(\int_\Omega u_n^2\,dx=1\) and \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_n|^2\,dx\to \lambda_1\). By boundedness in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\), weak compactness, compactness of the embedding \(H_0^1(\Omega)\hookrightarrow L^2(\Omega)\), and weak lower semicontinuity, obtain an admissible limit \(u_1\) attaining the infimum. Then apply the constrained Euler–Lagrange/Lagrange multiplier argument to the minimizer \(u_1\): there is \(\mu\) such that \(\int_\Omega \nabla u_1\cdot\nabla\varphi\,dx = \mu \int_\Omega u_1\varphi\,dx\) for all \(\varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega)\). Testing with \(\varphi=u_1\) and using \(\int_\Omega u_1^2\,dx=1\) and \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx=\lambda_1\), deduce \(\mu=\lambda_1\).

Display label: Attainment via compactness

Formalization priority: 1. Rationale: Highest-yield formal island: combines weak compactness, compact \(H_0^1\to L^2\) embedding, preservation of the \(L^2\)-constraint, and weak lower semicontinuity to prove attainment.

Informal statement:

If \((u_n)\subset H_0^1(\Omega)\) satisfies \(\int_\Omega u_n^2\,dx=1\) for all \(n\) and \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_n|^2\,dx\to \lambda_1\), then after passing to a subsequence there exists \(u_1\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) such that \(u_n\rightharpoonup u_1\) weakly in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\), \(u_n\to u_1\) strongly in \(L^2(\Omega)\), \(\int_\Omega u_1^2\,dx=1\), and \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx=\lambda_1\).

Informal proof:

Because \(\int_\Omega |\nabla u_n|^2\,dx\) stays bounded and \(\int_\Omega u_n^2\,dx=1\), the sequence is bounded in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\). Extract a subsequence converging weakly in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\) to some \(u_1\). By compactness of \(H_0^1(\Omega)\hookrightarrow L^2(\Omega)\), pass to a further subsequence with \(u_n\to u_1\) strongly in \(L^2(\Omega)\). Hence \(\int_\Omega u_1^2\,dx=\lim_n \int_\Omega u_n^2\,dx=1\), so \(u_1\) is admissible. By weak lower semicontinuity, \[ \int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx\le \liminf_{n\to\infty}\int_\Omega |\nabla u_n|^2\,dx=\lambda_1. \] Since \(u_1\) is admissible and \(\lambda_1\) is the infimum over admissible functions, also \(\lambda_1\le \int_\Omega |\nabla u_1|^2\,dx\). Therefore equality holds.

Informal proof:

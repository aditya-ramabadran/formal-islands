Pinsker's inequality via reduction to the Bernoulli case

Pinsker's inequality via reduction to the Bernoulli case. Let \((X,\mathcal F)\) be a measurable space, and let \(P\) and \(Q\) be probability measures on \((X,\mathcal F)\). Assume \(P\ll Q\), so that the Kullback–Leibler divergence \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q) := \int_X \log\!\left(\frac{dP}{dQ}\right)\,dP \] is well defined in \([0,\infty]\). Then \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge 2\,\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2, \] where \[ \|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}:=\sup_{A\in\mathcal F}|P(A)-Q(A)|. \] Equivalently, if \(\delta:=\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}\), then \[ D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge 2\delta^2. \] The key local scalar step is the Bernoulli inequality \[ p\log\!\left(\frac{p}{q}\right)+(1-p)\log\!\left(\frac{1-p}{1-q}\right)\ge 2(p-q)^2, \qquad 0<p,q<1, \] which appears after collapsing the general measures to a two-point partition.

Pinsker's inequality

Node id: root | Status: informal

Display label: Pinsker: $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge 2\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2$

Informal statement:

Let $P,Q$ be probability measures on $(X,\mathcal{F})$ with $P\ll Q$. Then $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge 2\,\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2$.

Informal proof:

Set $\delta:=\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}$. By the Hahn decomposition of $P-Q$, choose $A\in\mathcal{F}$ with $P(A)-Q(A)=\delta\ge 0$. Define the measurable map $T:=\mathbf{1}_A:X\to\{0,1\}$, and let $p:=P(A)$, $q:=Q(A)$, so $T_\#P=(p,1-p)$ and $T_\#Q=(q,1-q)$. By the data-processing inequality (DPI), $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge D_{\mathrm{KL}}(T_\#P\|T_\#Q)=p\log(p/q)+(1-p)\log((1-p)/(1-q))$. By the Bernoulli scalar inequality applied with $p-q=\delta$, the right-hand side is $\ge 2(p-q)^2=2\delta^2$. Hence $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge 2\delta^2=2\|P-Q\|_{\mathrm{TV}}^2$.

Data-processing inequality for KL divergence

Node id: dpi | Status: informal

Display label: DPI: $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge D_{\mathrm{KL}}(T_\#P\|T_\#Q)$

Informal statement:

Let $P,Q$ be probability measures on $(X,\mathcal{F})$ with $P\ll Q$, and let $T:(X,\mathcal{F})\to(Y,\mathcal{G})$ be any measurable map. Then $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge D_{\mathrm{KL}}(T_\#P\|T_\#Q)$.

Informal proof:

This is the standard information-monotonicity property of relative entropy: applying a measurable map coarsens the $\sigma$-algebra and cannot increase relative entropy. It follows from Jensen's inequality (log-sum inequality) or from the chain rule for KL and non-negativity of conditional KL. In the binary pushforward case $T=\mathbf{1}_A$, this specializes to $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\|Q)\ge p\log(p/q)+(1-p)\log((1-p)/(1-q))$ with $p=P(A)$, $q=Q(A)$.

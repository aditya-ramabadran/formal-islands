Informal statement:

Let \(d \ge 1\) and let \(f : \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{C}\) be sufficiently regular and sufficiently decaying at infinity so that the integrations by parts below are justified, with \(f \in H^1(\mathbb{R}^d)\) and \(x f \in L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)\). Then \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 \le \frac{2}{d}\,\|x f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}\,\|\nabla f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}. \]

Informal proof:

Start from \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 = \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |f(x)|^2\,dx = \frac1d \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} (\operatorname{div} x) |f(x)|^2\,dx, \] using \(\operatorname{div}(x)=d\). Integrating by parts and discarding the boundary term by the decay assumptions gives \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 = -\frac1d \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} x\cdot \nabla(|f(x)|^2)\,dx. \] Next, \[ \nabla(|f|^2)=\nabla(f\overline f)=(\nabla f)\overline f+f\nabla\overline f, \] so \[ x\cdot \nabla(|f|^2)=2\,\operatorname{Re}(x\cdot \nabla f\,\overline f). \] Hence \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 = -\frac{2}{d}\operatorname{Re}\int_{\mathbb{R}^d} x\cdot \nabla f(x)\,\overline{f(x)}\,dx. \] Taking absolute values and using \(|\operatorname{Re} z|\le |z|\), \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 \le \frac{2}{d}\int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |x|\,|\nabla f(x)|\,|f(x)|\,dx. \] Apply Cauchy--Schwarz to \(|x||f(x)|\) and \(|\nabla f(x)|\): \[ \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |x|\,|\nabla f(x)|\,|f(x)|\,dx \le \left(\int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |x|^2 |f(x)|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2} \left(\int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |\nabla f(x)|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2}. \] This is \[ \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} |x|\,|\nabla f|\,|f| \le \|x f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}\,\|\nabla f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}. \] Combining the two estimates yields \[ \|f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}^2 \le \frac{2}{d}\,\|x f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}\,\|\nabla f\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R}^d)}, \] as claimed.

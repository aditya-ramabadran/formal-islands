Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram

Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram. Let \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a bounded domain with sufficiently regular boundary, and let \(f \in L^2(\Omega)\). Then there exists a unique \(u \in H_0^1(\Omega)\) such that \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u \cdot \nabla v\,dx = \int_\Omega f v\,dx \qquad \text{for all } v \in H_0^1(\Omega). \] Moreover, \[ \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)} \le C \|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}. \]

Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram

Node id: n0 | Status: informal

Display label: Poisson weak solution via Lax–Milgram

Informal statement:

With \(U:=H_0^1(\Omega)\), define \(a(u,v):=\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\) and \(\ell(v):=\int_\Omega f v\,dx\). Then there exists a unique \(u\in U\) such that \(a(u,v)=\ell(v)\) for all \(v\in U\), i.e. \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u \cdot \nabla v\,dx = \int_\Omega f v\,dx \qquad \forall v\in H_0^1(\Omega), \] and \(\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)} \le C\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\).

Informal proof:

Check the hypotheses of Lax–Milgram on \(U=H_0^1(\Omega)\): the form \(a\) is bilinear, bounded, and coercive, and the functional \(\ell\) is continuous. Then apply the Lax–Milgram theorem to obtain existence and uniqueness of \(u\in U\) with \(a(u,v)=\ell(v)\) for all \(v\in U\). Unwinding the definitions gives the weak formulation. The standard a priori estimate from Lax–Milgram yields \(\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)} \le C\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\).

Boundedness of the Dirichlet bilinear form

Node id: n1 | Status: informal

Display label: boundedness of \(a\)

Informal statement:

For all \(u,v\in H_0^1(\Omega)\), \[ |a(u,v)|=\left|\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\right| \le \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\,\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}. \] Hence \(a\) is bounded on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\times H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Informal proof:

Apply Cauchy–Schwarz to the integral of \(\nabla u\cdot\nabla v\): \[ \left|\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\right| \le \left(\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2} \left(\int_\Omega |\nabla v|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2}. \] On \(H_0^1(\Omega)\), this is exactly the \(H_0^1\)-norm bound used here.

Coercivity of the Dirichlet bilinear form

For all \(u\in H_0^1(\Omega)\), \[ a(u,u)=\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx=\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}^2. \] Therefore \(a\) is coercive on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Continuity of the load functional

Formalization priority: 1. Rationale: Most valuable local formal island: it packages the concrete estimate for \(\ell\), combining Cauchy–Schwarz with Poincaré, and removes a real analytic obligation in the Lax–Milgram setup.

Informal statement:

For all \(v\in H_0^1(\Omega)\), \[ |\ell(v)|=\left|\int_\Omega f v\,dx\right| \le C_P\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\,\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}. \] Hence \(\ell\) is continuous on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Informal proof:

First apply Cauchy–Schwarz: \[ \left|\int_\Omega f v\,dx\right|\le \|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\|v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}. \] Then use the Poincaré inequality on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\), \[ \|v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\le C_P\|\nabla v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}, \] to obtain the claimed bound in terms of \(\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\).

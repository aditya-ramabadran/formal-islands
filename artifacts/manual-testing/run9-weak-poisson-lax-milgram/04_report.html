Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram

Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram. Let \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a bounded domain with sufficiently regular boundary, and let \(f \in L^2(\Omega)\). Then there exists a unique \(u \in H_0^1(\Omega)\) such that \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u \cdot \nabla v\,dx = \int_\Omega f v\,dx \qquad \text{for all } v \in H_0^1(\Omega). \] Moreover, \[ \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)} \le C \|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}. \]

Weak formulation of the Poisson problem via Lax–Milgram

Node id: n1 | Status: formal_failed

Display label: Poisson weak solution exists uniquely

Formalization priority: 3. Rationale: Once the two bounds are formalized, the remaining step is a clean application of Lax–Milgram plus its standard norm estimate, making the root theorem a reasonable final assembly target.

Informal statement:

With \(U:=H_0^1(\Omega)\), there exists a unique \(u\in U\) such that \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx=\int_\Omega f v\,dx \qquad \forall v\in U, \] and \(\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\le C\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\).

Informal proof:

Define \(a(u,v):=\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\) and \(\ell(v):=\int_\Omega f v\,dx\). By the auxiliary claims, \(a\) is a bilinear, bounded, coercive form on \(U\), and \(\ell\) is a continuous linear functional on \(U\). Therefore the hypotheses of the Lax–Milgram theorem are satisfied, so there exists a unique \(u\in U\) such that \(a(u,v)=\ell(v)\) for all \(v\in U\). Writing this out gives exactly \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx=\int_\Omega f v\,dx \qquad \forall v\in H_0^1(\Omega). \] The standard Lax–Milgram estimate then yields \(\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\le C\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\), where \(C\) depends on the continuity bound for \(\ell\) and the coercivity constant of \(a\).

The energy form is bounded and coercive on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\)

Node id: n2 | Status: candidate_formal

Display label: Boundedness and coercivity of \(a\)

Formalization priority: 1. Rationale: High-yield local formal island: a concrete inequality plus the exact coercivity identity \(a(u,u)=\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}^2\), which carries the main operator-side verification for Lax–Milgram.

Informal statement:

For \(a(u,v):=\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\) on \(U=H_0^1(\Omega)\), the form \(a\) is bilinear, bounded, and coercive. In fact, \[ |a(u,v)|\le \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}, \qquad a(u,u)=\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}^2. \]

Informal proof:

Bilinearity is immediate from linearity of the integral and the gradient. For boundedness, apply Cauchy–Schwarz: \[ |a(u,v)|=\left|\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx\right| \le \left(\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2} \left(\int_\Omega |\nabla v|^2\,dx\right)^{1/2}. \] Since on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\) the chosen norm is the gradient norm, this is exactly \[ |a(u,v)|\le \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}. \] For coercivity, \[ a(u,u)=\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx=\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}^2, \] so \(a(u,u)\ge 1\cdot \|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}^2\). Thus the coercivity constant is \(\alpha=1\).

The load functional is continuous on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\)

Node id: n3 | Status: candidate_formal

Display label: Continuity of \(\ell\)

Formalization priority: 2. Rationale: Concrete functional estimate combining Cauchy–Schwarz with Poincaré; this is the main data-side bound and is plausibly reusable in nearby elliptic weak-form arguments.

Informal statement:

For \(\ell(v):=\int_\Omega f v\,dx\) with \(f\in L^2(\Omega)\), the functional \(\ell\) is linear and continuous on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\). More precisely, \[ |\ell(v)|\le C_P\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}. \]

Informal proof:

Linearity is immediate from linearity of the integral. For continuity, Cauchy–Schwarz gives \[ |\ell(v)|=\left|\int_\Omega f v\,dx\right| \le \|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\|v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}. \] Using the Poincaré inequality on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\), \[ \|v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\le C_P\|\nabla v\|_{L^2(\Omega)}=C_P\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}, \] we obtain \[ |\ell(v)|\le C_P\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}\|v\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)}. \] Hence \(\ell\) is a bounded linear functional on \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

# Boundedness of the bilinear form a

We formalize the boundedness of the bilinear form a(f, g) := ∫_{ℝ^d} f(x) g(x) dx
on L²(ℝ^d) × L²(ℝ^d), which models the Dirichlet bilinear form
  a(u, v) = ∫_Ω ∇u · ∇v dx
on H₀¹(Ω) × H₀¹(Ω), where the H₀¹-norm equals the L²-norm of the gradient.

The key estimate is |a(f, g)| ≤ ‖f‖_{L²} · ‖g‖_{L²}, which follows from the
Cauchy–Schwarz inequality for the L² inner product.

Since Mathlib does not yet contain Sobolev spaces H₀¹(Ω), we work with L²(ℝ^d, ℝ)
equipped with Lebesgue measure. The bilinear form a is defined as the integral
∫ f · g, which is the L² inner product.  This faithfully captures the structure
used in the Lax–Milgram proof of the Poisson problem: the H₀¹ inner product
⟪u, v⟫_{H₀¹} = ∫ ∇u · ∇v dx is precisely the L² inner product of the gradients.

Display label: Main theorem (Lax–Milgram application)

Informal statement:

There exists a unique $u \in H_0^1(\Omega)$ satisfying $\int_\Omega \nabla u \cdot \nabla v\,dx = \int_\Omega f v\,dx$ for all $v \in H_0^1(\Omega)$, and $\|u\|_{H_0^1(\Omega)} \le C\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}$.

Informal proof:

Set $U := H_0^1(\Omega)$, $a(u,v) := \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx$, and $\ell(v) := \int_\Omega fv\,dx$. $a$ is bilinear and, by the boundedness lemma, satisfies $|a(u,v)| \le \|u\|_U\|v\|_U$ with constant $M = 1$. $a$ is coercive: $a(u,u) = \int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx = \|u\|_{H_0^1}^2$, so the coercivity constant is $\alpha = 1$. $\ell$ is linear and, by the continuity lemma (using Poincaré), bounded on $U$. All hypotheses of the Lax–Milgram theorem are satisfied. Therefore there exists a unique $u \in U$ such that $a(u,v) = \ell(v)$ for all $v \in U$, i.e., $\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla v\,dx = \int_\Omega fv\,dx$ for all $v \in H_0^1(\Omega)$. The standard Lax–Milgram estimate gives $\|u\|_U \le \frac{1}{\alpha}\|\ell\|_{U^*} \le C_P\|f\|_{L^2(\Omega)}$.

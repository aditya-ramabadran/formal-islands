Finite-time blow-up for the semilinear heat equation by the energy method

Finite-time blow-up for the semilinear heat equation by the energy method. Let \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a bounded domain, and let \(u(t,x)\) be a sufficiently regular solution of \[ u_t = \Delta u + |u|^{p-1}u \qquad \text{in } \Omega, \] with homogeneous Dirichlet boundary condition \[ u=0 \qquad \text{on } \partial\Omega, \] for some \(p>1\). Define the energy \[ E(t) = \frac12 \int_\Omega |\nabla u(t,x)|^2\,dx - \frac{1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u(t,x)|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Assume the initial energy is nonpositive: \[ E(0)\le 0. \] Then the quantity \[ Y(t):=\int_\Omega |u(t,x)|^2\,dx \] satisfies a differential inequality that forces finite-time blow-up.

Finite-time blow-up from nonpositive initial energy

Node id: n0 | Status: informal

Display label: Blow-up theorem

Informal statement:

Under the assumptions above, the energy is nonincreasing and nonpositive for all times of existence, one obtains \[ Y'(t)\ge c\,Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}} \] for some \(c>0\), and since \(\frac{p+1}{2}>1\), comparison with the ODE \(z'=c z^{\frac{p+1}{2}}\) implies finite-time blow-up of \(Y(t)\).

Informal proof:

Differentiate the energy and integrate by parts: \[ \frac{dE}{dt} = \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot \nabla u_t\,dx- \int_\Omega |u|^{p-1}u\,u_t\,dx = -\int_\Omega (\Delta u+|u|^{p-1}u)u_t\,dx. \] Using the PDE \(u_t=\Delta u+|u|^{p-1}u\), this gives \[ E'(t)=-\int_\Omega |u_t|^2\,dx\le 0, \] so \(E(t)\le E(0)\le 0\). Next, \[ \frac12 Y'(t)=\int_\Omega u u_t\,dx =\int_\Omega u\Delta u\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx =-\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Rewrite the right-hand side using the definition of \(E(t)\): \[ \frac12 Y'(t) =-2E(t)+\frac{p-1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Since \(E(t)\le 0\), \[ \frac12 Y'(t)\ge \frac{p-1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Because \(\Omega\) is bounded and \(p+1>2\), Hölder yields \[ \int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx\ge C_\Omega\Bigl(\int_\Omega |u|^2\,dx\Bigr)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}=C_\Omega Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] Hence \[ Y'(t)\ge c Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] Since \(\frac{p+1}{2}>1\), any positive solution of the comparison ODE blows up in finite time, so \(Y(t)\) cannot stay finite for all time. Thus the solution blows up in finite time.

Energy dissipation identity

Node id: n1 | Status: candidate_formal

Display label: Energy dissipation

Formalization priority: 2. Rationale: High-yield formal lemma: the energy dissipation computation is standard but nontrivial, involving differentiation, integration by parts, and substitution of the PDE.

Informal statement:

The energy satisfies \[ E'(t)=-\int_\Omega |u_t|^2\,dx\le 0. \] In particular, \(E(t)\le E(0)\le 0\).

Informal proof:

Differentiate \[ E(t)=\frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx-\frac{1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Then \[ E'(t)=\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot \nabla u_t\,dx-\int_\Omega |u|^{p-1}u\,u_t\,dx. \] Integrate the first term by parts, using \(u=0\) on \(\partial\Omega\), to get \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot \nabla u_t\,dx=-\int_\Omega (\Delta u)u_t\,dx. \] Therefore \[ E'(t)=-\int_\Omega (\Delta u+|u|^{p-1}u)u_t\,dx. \] Substitute the equation \(u_t=\Delta u+|u|^{p-1}u\) and obtain \[ E'(t)=-\int_\Omega |u_t|^2\,dx. \] This is nonpositive, so the energy is nonincreasing.

Differential inequality for \(Y(t)\)

Node id: n2 | Status: informal

Display label: Mass inequality

Informal statement:

The \(L^2\)-mass satisfies \[ \frac12 Y'(t)=-\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx =-2E(t)+\frac{p-1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx, \] and hence, since \(E(t)\le 0\), \[ \frac12 Y'(t)\ge \frac{p-1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \]

Informal proof:

Differentiate \(Y(t)=\int_\Omega |u|^2\,dx\): \[ \frac12 Y'(t)=\int_\Omega u u_t\,dx. \] Use the PDE: \[ \frac12 Y'(t)=\int_\Omega u\Delta u\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Integrating by parts and using \(u=0\) on \(\partial\Omega\) gives \[ \int_\Omega u\Delta u\,dx=-\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx. \] So \[ \frac12 Y'(t)=-\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] From \[ E(t)=\frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx-\frac{1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx, \] one rewrites \[ -\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^2\,dx+\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx =-2E(t)+\frac{p-1}{p+1}\int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx. \] Since node \(n1\) gives \(E(t)\le 0\), the lower bound follows.

Bounded-domain lower bound and ODE comparison

Node id: n3 | Status: candidate_formal

Display label: Norm lower bound + ODE

Formalization priority: 3. Rationale: Useful local package combining the bounded-domain \(L^{p+1}\)-to-\(L^2\) lower bound with the scalar ODE comparison; this closes the argument once \(n2\) is available.

Informal statement:

Because \(\Omega\) is bounded and \(p+1>2\), there is \(C_\Omega>0\) such that \[ \int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx\ge C_\Omega Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] Combining with node \(n2\) yields \[ Y'(t)\ge c Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] Since \(\frac{p+1}{2}>1\), comparison with \(z'=c z^{\frac{p+1}{2}}\) forces finite-time blow-up.

Informal proof:

Use the standard finite-measure comparison between \(L^{p+1}(\Omega)\) and \(L^2(\Omega)\): on a bounded domain, \[ \|u\|_{L^{p+1}}\ge C_\Omega \|u\|_{L^2}. \] Raising to the power \(p+1\) gives \[ \int_\Omega |u|^{p+1}\,dx\ge C_\Omega \left(\int_\Omega |u|^2\,dx\right)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}=C_\Omega Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] Insert this into the inequality from node \(n2\) to obtain \[ Y'(t)\ge c Y(t)^{\frac{p+1}{2}}. \] For \(\alpha=\frac{p+1}{2}>1\), solutions of \(z'=c z^\alpha\) with positive initial data blow up in finite time, and the standard comparison principle implies the same for \(Y(t)\).

/--
A concrete algebraic formalization of the `L^2`-mass differential inequality used in the
energy-method proof of finite-time blow-up for the semilinear heat equation.
-/
theorem differential_inequality_for_Y
    {p Y' gradIntegral nonlinIntegral E : ℝ}
    (hp : 1 < p)
    (hY : (1 / 2 : ℝ) * Y' = -gradIntegral + nonlinIntegral)
    (hE : E = (1 / 2 : ℝ) * gradIntegral - (1 / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral)
    (hEnonpos : E ≤ 0) :
    (1 / 2 : ℝ) * Y' = -2 * E + ((p - 1) / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral ∧
      (1 / 2 : ℝ) * Y' ≥ ((p - 1) / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral := by
  have hp1 : p + 1 ≠ 0 := by
    linarith
  have hrewrite :
      -2 * E + ((p - 1) / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral = -gradIntegral + nonlinIntegral := by
    rw [hE]
    field_simp [hp1]
    ring
  constructor
  · rw [hY]
    symm
    exact hrewrite
  · have hnonneg : 0 ≤ -2 * E := by
      nlinarith
    calc
      (1 / 2 : ℝ) * Y'
          = -2 * E + ((p - 1) / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral := by
              rw [hY]
              symm
              exact hrewrite
      _ ≥ ((p - 1) / (p + 1)) * nonlinIntegral := by
          linarith

end FormalIslands

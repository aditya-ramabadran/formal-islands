Weak solution equals unique minimizer of the Dirichlet energy

Weak solution equals unique minimizer of the Dirichlet energy. Let \(\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d\) be a bounded domain with sufficiently regular boundary, and let \(f \in L^2(\Omega)\). Define \[ J(v) := \frac12 \int_\Omega |\nabla v|^2 \, dx - \int_\Omega f v \, dx \qquad \text{for } v \in H_0^1(\Omega). \] Then \(u \in H_0^1(\Omega)\) satisfies \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u \cdot \nabla \varphi \, dx = \int_\Omega f \varphi \, dx \qquad \text{for every } \varphi \in H_0^1(\Omega) \] if and only if \(u\) is the unique minimizer of \(J\) over \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Informal statement:

For \(u\in H_0^1(\Omega)\), the weak formulation \[ \int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot \nabla \varphi\,dx=\int_\Omega f\varphi\,dx\quad \forall\,\varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega) \] is equivalent to \(u\) being the unique minimizer of \[ J(v)=\frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla v|^2\,dx-\int_\Omega f v\,dx \] over \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Informal proof:

Combine the two directions: a weak solution satisfies the exact energy gap identity, hence minimizes uniquely; conversely, a minimizer has vanishing first variation along every direction, which is exactly the weak equation.

Energy gap identity from the weak equation

Node id: n1 | Status: formal_failed

Display label: Gap identity

Formalization priority: 1. Rationale: Best formal island: the exact energy decomposition is compact, reusable, and directly yields both minimality and uniqueness from the weak equation.

Informal statement:

If \(u\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) satisfies the weak equation, then for every \(v\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) one has \[ J(v)=J(u)+\frac12\int_\Omega |\nabla(v-u)|^2\,dx. \] In particular, \(J(v)\ge J(u)\), with equality only when \(v=u\) in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

Informal proof:

Write \(v=u+w\) with \(w\in H_0^1(\Omega)\), expand \(|\nabla(u+w)|^2\), and regroup terms as \(J(u)\) plus a linear bracket plus \(\frac12\int |\nabla w|^2\). The weak formulation with test function \(\varphi=w\) kills the linear bracket, leaving the exact gap identity. Nonnegativity of the last term gives minimality, and vanishing of the gap forces \(\nabla(v-u)=0\), hence \(v=u\) in \(H_0^1(\Omega)\).

First variation of \(J\) along lines

Node id: n2 | Status: candidate_formal

Display label: First variation

Formalization priority: 2. Rationale: Second key formal island: the one-dimensional variation argument is the cleanest route from minimizer to weak formulation and is easy to formalize locally.

Informal statement:

If \(u\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) is a minimizer of \(J\), then for every \(\varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) the function \(g(t)=J(u+t\varphi)\) has \(g'(0)=0\), and \[ g'(0)=\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot \nabla \varphi\,dx-\int_\Omega f\varphi\,dx. \] Therefore \(u\) satisfies the weak formulation.

Informal proof:

Fix \(\varphi\in H_0^1(\Omega)\) and consider the one-variable restriction \(g(t)=J(u+t\varphi)\). Since \(u\) minimizes \(J\), \(t=0\) is a minimum of \(g\), so \(g'(0)=0\). Expanding \(|\nabla(u+t\varphi)|^2\) gives a quadratic polynomial in \(t\), whose derivative at \(0\) is exactly \(\int_\Omega \nabla u\cdot\nabla\varphi\,dx-\int_\Omega f\varphi\,dx\). Setting this equal to zero yields the weak equation.

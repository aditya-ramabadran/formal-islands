Informal statement:

Let \(u,v\) be sufficiently regular solutions of the heat equation on the interval \((0,L)\) for times \(t \in [0,T]\): \[ u_t = u_{xx}, \qquad v_t = v_{xx}, \] with the same initial data \(u(x,0)=v(x,0)\), and the same homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions \(u(0,t)=u(L,t)=v(0,t)=v(L,t)=0\). Then \(u=v\) on \([0,L]\times[0,T]\).

Informal proof:

Set \[ w=u-v. \] Then \(w\) satisfies \[ w_t=w_{xx} \] on \((0,L)\times(0,T)\), with initial data \[ w(x,0)=0 \] and boundary conditions \[ w(0,t)=w(L,t)=0. \] Define the energy \[ E(t)=\frac12\int_0^L w(x,t)^2\,dx. \] By the energy dissipation lemma, we have \(E'(t) \le 0\). Therefore \(E(t)\) is nonincreasing in time. At \(t=0\), we have \(w(x,0)=0\), so \[ E(0)=\frac12\int_0^L 0^2\,dx=0. \] Since \(E(t)\ge 0\) by definition and \(E\) is nonincreasing, we must have \[ E(t)=0 \] for all \(t\in[0,T]\). Thus \[ \int_0^L w(x,t)^2\,dx = 0 \] for all \(t\), so \(w(x,t)=0\) for all \(x,t\). Hence \(u=v\).

Formalization priority: 1. Rationale: The energy dissipation inequality contains the core calculus steps: differentiation under the integral sign and integration by parts. It isolates the most technically demanding part of the proof into a highly concrete, well-scoped formal island.

Informal statement:

Let \(w\) be a sufficiently regular solution to \(w_t = w_{xx}\) on \((0,L)\times(0,T)\) with boundary conditions \(w(0,t)=w(L,t)=0\). Define the energy \[ E(t)=\frac12\int_0^L w(x,t)^2\,dx. \] Then \(E'(t) \le 0\).

Informal proof:

Because \(w\) is sufficiently regular, we may differentiate under the integral sign: \[ E'(t)=\int_0^L w(x,t)\,w_t(x,t)\,dx. \] Using the equation \(w_t=w_{xx}\), we get \[ E'(t)=\int_0^L w\,w_{xx}\,dx. \] Now integrate by parts in \(x\): \[ \int_0^L w\,w_{xx}\,dx = \bigl[w\,w_x\bigr]_{0}^{L} - \int_0^L |w_x|^2\,dx. \] Since \(w(0,t)=w(L,t)=0\), the boundary term vanishes, so \[ E'(t)=-\int_0^L |w_x(x,t)|^2\,dx \le 0. \]

# Uniqueness for the heat equation by the energy method

We prove that if two continuous functions u, v on [0,L] √ó [0,T] satisfy the same

  E(t) = ‚ąę‚āÄŠīł (u(x,t) - v(x,t))¬≤ dx

1. E(0) = 0 from the initial data.

4. Therefore E(t) = 0 for all t.
